# Product

## Register

product

> Note: Both the app (dashboards, workflows, data tables) and the marketing surface (landing, pricing, auth pages) matter equally. Register defaults to `product`; override to `brand` for landing-page-specific tasks.

## Users

Sales professionals and growth/marketing teams hunting for prospects, managing contact pipelines, and running outreach campaigns. They are task-focused and move fast: they open the app to find leads, act on them, and measure results. Enterprise users also manage large contact databases and scheduled automations. Non-technical users are a real segment — the UI must guide without requiring training.

## Product Purpose

Leadfinder is a lead generation and outreach management SaaS. It lets users discover and score leads with AI, manage contacts through a pipeline (New → Contacted → Converted → Archived), build reusable filters and automations, and run campaigns across Email, SMS, and LinkedIn. Success means a user can go from zero to a live campaign without friction or confusion.

## Brand Personality

Bold, Modern, Ambitious. The voice is confident and forward-looking — a tool that believes it can outperform the legacy CRMs. Not flashy or loud; the boldness comes from clarity and intentionality, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- **Neon/hacker aesthetic** (dark + garish glow effects, cyberpunk overload) — the dark theme must feel polished and purposeful, not terminal-themed.
- **Bloomberg terminal data-overload** — density is fine when earned, but every element must have breathing room; intimidating walls of data are a failure.
- By extension: generic enterprise-bland (HubSpot beige, Salesforce corporate blue) should also be avoided, as Leadfinder is meant to feel like a step forward from those tools.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence without clutter** — Bold choices in typography, color, and hierarchy. Decoration only when it earns its place. Whitespace is intentional, not empty.
2. **Signal over noise** — Surface what the user needs right now. Suppress secondary information until it's needed. Every screen has one primary action.
3. **Marketing and product, one voice** — The landing pages and the app interior share the same visual vocabulary. Switching between them should feel continuous, not jarring.
4. **Ambition serves the task** — Modern aesthetics are a means, not an end. Every design choice must make the user's job easier or faster, not slower.
5. **Speed is design** — Perceived and actual performance are design concerns. Transitions, loading states, and interactions should feel snappy and intentional.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Ensure sufficient color contrast on both dark and light themes. Support `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animated elements. The product serves a broad range of users including non-technical ones; clarity and legibility are accessibility concerns, not just contrast ratios.
